Culture Kozhikode

Kozhikode has a strong tradition in art and literature. The city hosts various cultural events and festivals, showcasing local talent in music, dance, and drama. It has also been home to eminent poets, writers, and artists, contributing significantly to the Malayalam literary world.Kozhikode, also known as Calicut, has a rich and diverse culture that's a beautiful blend of history, traditions, and modern influences. The city has a strong historical significance and was an important trading hub for spices during ancient times, attracting various cultures and contributing to its unique identity. Mappilappattu

Mappila pattu is a genre of Muslim folk songs from the Malabar region of Kerala. Kozhikode Aboobacker and VM Kutty are two notable Mappila pattu singers from the Kozhikode district of Kerala. Kolkali

With a history of about 200 years, Kolkali is a folk art form performed in the North Malabar region of Kerala. It is said to have drawn elements from Kalaripayattu, a martial art practised in Kerala and Tamil Nadu. In Kolkali, each of the performers holds a stick and moves in circle, striking the sticks and keeping rhythm to the accompanying music. As the music gradually rises in pitch, the pace increases till the performance reaches its climax. The circle expands and contracts as the dance progresses. Every dancer moves in accordance with a close pattern, without touching each other or missing a beat. As the dance advances to the end, the dancers move in vigorous steps, with sweeping movements and turns. Kolkali is widespread among the Dravidians. It has exerted a great influence in the folk dance forms of Bengal, Gujarat, Punjab, and Maharashtra. In Tamil Nadu, this art form is referred to as Kolattam and in Andhra Pradesh, as Kolamu. Kolkali is now a popular item in Kerala School Youth Festival, which is supposed to be the biggest cultural event in Asia.

Thirayattam

Thirayattam is a tribal art form that combines dance, acting routines, songs, instrumental music, face writing, martial arts and rituals. The unique rituals, costumes and artistic performances differentiate Thirayattam from other art forms. It is usually enacted in courtyards called kaavukal in village shrines of Kozhikode and Malappuram districts. The dance which is staged in the courtyards is accompanied by musical instruments such as Chenda melam and a warm light
